Holbox Discovery

Isn’t life great when it’s lo-fi and low-rise? That’s the attitude on friendly Isla Holbox (hol-bosh) with its sandy streets, colorful Caribbean buildings, and blissful sun-drunk dogs. Holbox is a welcome refuge for anyone looking to just get away from it all.
Discover the paradisiacal beaches of Holbox Island, a natural space on the northern coast of Yucatan characterized by its spectacular landscapes and its great diversity of birds.

Sian Kaan Jeep or Deluxe

The Sian Kaan Biosphere Reserve (meaning where the sky is born) has been an UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1986. The national park stretches over 120km and covers more than 1.3 million acres of coastal jungle.
During this wonderful excursion you will drive a jeep from Tulum to Punta Allen through the lush jungle on a bumpy coast road in one of the largest eco reserves in the world to Punta Allen. From there begins the incredible boat ride.
You will experience a friendly encounter with local marine life (dolphins, starfish and sea turtles if conditions allow), swim in a natural pool, go snorkeling along the Mesoamerican reef, observe many birds at bird island and enjoy a delicious buffet overlooking the Caribbean Sea. This excursion is also possible as a Deluxe version where instead of driving a jeep, you will be taken by boat to Punta Allen! 

Tulum Ruins

Tulum, considered by many as the most beautiful of the Mayan Ruins sites in this area. Tulum is small but exquisitely poised on the fifteen-meter-high cliffs above the azure waters of the Caribbean Sea. Tulum is not the largest or most impressive Mayan Ruins, but thanks to the awe-inspiring setting, it sticks in the memory like no other. It is also the nearest to Playa del Carmen and Cancun. A great option for visiting Tulum Mayan Ruins is to book this as a combination tour where you will start the day with a professional guided tour at the Mayan Ruins in combination with some fun and refreshing snorkeling at at a coral reef.

Contoy island & Isla Mujeres

On this excursion you will be taken to two most beautiful and best known islands in the Yucatán Peninsula Isla Contoy and Isla Mujeres. Contoy Island is a national park, with spectacular beaches, and a protected natural reserve since it is a very important shelter and nesting area for maritime birds. Only 200 visitors per day are allowed on this island . For it’s fantastic biodiversity, Contoy Island is a magnificent spotting place for pelicans, storks, sparrows, cormorants, and many other bird species. Isla Mujeres (spanish for Island of the women) is a delightful island with tons of history. Here the Gulf of Mexico and the Carribean Sea meet. This island will rejuvenate your soul!

Whale Shark Discovery

Whale Shark watching and swimming, just a few feet away from them! Anunforgettable experience! Whale Sharks are the largest fish in the world. They are totally harmless and peaceful as they only eat plankton and small fishes. Because of the warm waters and the big amounts of plankton, this area makes it the ideal Whale Shark habitat from mid-May to September each year.
Our friendly and knowledgeable guides will make your Whale Shark watching and swimming experience the highlight of your vacation! After the swimming with the Whale Sharks, we will take you snorkeling at another beautiful location, the coral reef in Isla Mujeres. We'll then bring you to Playa Norte, named one of the most beautiful beaches in the world, where we'll have lunch and fun!

Magic Bacalar

The lagoon of Bacalar, also known as the lagoon with the seven colors, is an immense freshwater lagoon that joins the sea through the mangroves. The lagoon is formed by 4 open cenotes, which are very deep. Bacalar is a former pirates’ haunt, just like Isla Holbox in the north of the peninsula. It is very well known for the different shades of blue it reflects, from deep blue to turquoise, emerald etc. It feels like Bora Bora. We offer an exclusive, beautiful and fun day excursion where you will see and learn so many things about this magical place!
